The History of Skyline House

From construction to community — over 50 years of highrise living in Northern Virginia.

Skyline House has been a landmark of the Bailey's Crossroads community since the early 1970s. What began as a modern vision for urban highrise living has grown into a vibrant, close-knit community of owners and residents who take pride in their home.

1971
Construction begins
Ground is broken on the 18-story residential tower at 3711 S. George Mason Drive in Falls Church, Virginia, designed to bring modern highrise living to the Northern Virginia suburbs.
1973
Doors open
Skyline House welcomes its first residents. The 272-unit building offers stunning views of the Washington D.C. skyline and convenient access to the capital region.
1980s
Conversion to condominiums
Skyline House transitions from rental to condominium ownership, forming the Skyline House Unit Owners Association (SHUOA) to govern the community.
1990s
Community programs established
The Activities Committee, Good Neighbors program, and Welcome Wagon are established, creating the community spirit that Skyline House is known for today.
2000s
Major renovations
Significant upgrades to common areas, mechanical systems, and building infrastructure ensure Skyline House remains a premier address in Northern Virginia.
Today
A thriving community
Skyline House continues to be one of the most sought-after addresses in Fairfax County, with an active community, well-maintained facilities, and residents proud to call it home.

"Skyline House has always been more than just a building — it's a community where neighbors become friends and residents take pride in their shared home."

— Skyline House Unit Owners Association
